// Speakers

Serverless Architecture for Developers – Shekhar Gulati (Xebia)

Introduction: Shekhar Gulati has around 12 years of experience in technology consulting and Agile software development. Last couple of years, I have worked on building a deployment automation tool in Java, Scala, and Akka. Before that, I worked as a technology evangelist for Red Hat speaking and writing about cloud computing ecosystem.

Abstract: Serverless is an overloaded word. It means different things depending on the context. This session focuses on how Serverless compute enables us to build applications that are scalable, fault-tolerant, and event-driven. Serverless applications tend to be composed of small services aka Microservices. Thus, giving you all the benefits of Microservices.

Introduction to Azure AI – Brijraj Singh (Microsoft)

Introduction: Brij is a Sr. Technology Evangelist (Open Source solutions) with Microsoft. Brij has a decade worth of experience in both Microsoft technologies as well as with open source technologies. Brij has architected Highly scalable and reliable solutions using Open source technologies. Brij works with a number of startups to help them shape their products over cloud. Brij is also a local Docker Mentor.

Abstract: Everyone is talking about AI. The world of application programming is changing for the good with the advent of new AI services in the cloud. Implementing AI isn’t a complex science any more, reserved for scientists. We have made it simple with Azure, So simple that anyone who writes code can use it. During this session we’ll discuss the Azure cognitive services and understand how to introduce AI capabilities in your solutions.

Journey from COMX to AWS for Periscope Applications – Kiran Pratap (Thoughtworks)

Introduction: I am a developer working with ThoughtWorks ​and has about 16 years of industry experience. I have been associated with McKinsey Solutions since the beginning of 2015. I had worked on Periscope Modules like Periscope for Retail (Promotion Planning & Markdown) and has also been involved in the implementation of the solution for deploying Periscope applications into AWS. As of today we have 5 Solutions deployed into confidential environment on AWS.

Abstract: In this talk, we will be covering the migration of applications of Periscope Suite from COMX to AWS. This talk will cover various choices made:

  1. Security & Client Isolation
  2. Scaling
  3. Versioning
  4. Cost Considerations
